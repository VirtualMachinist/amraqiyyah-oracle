\# PART 1: LANGUAGE OVERVIEW

  

\#\# 1.1 FOUNDATIONAL INFORMATION

  

\*\*Language Name:\*\* Soulaani (ⲤⲰⲖⲀⲚⲒ / Swl·ny)  

\*\*Endonym:\*\* “Soul Language”  

\*\*Language Family:\*\* Constructed language (Soul Autochthonous American)  

\*\*Primary Script:\*\* Coptic alphabet  

\*\*Secondary Script:\*\* Latin romanization

  

\*\*Genetic Influences:\*\*

  

\- \*\*Morphological Foundation:\*\* Ancient Egyptian (template morphology, suffix/prefix conjugation)

\- \*\*Lexical Sources:\*\* Ancient Egyptian, Muskogean, Cherokee, Arabic, Hebrew, Latin, Greek

\- \*\*Grammatical Innovations:\*\* Formalized African American Vernacular English (AAVE) features

  

\*\*Geographic Context:\*\* Soul Land - The Southeast & Mid-Atlantic USA; ancestral home and location of ethnogenesis 

\*\*Speaker Demographics:\*\* Soulaan: Foundational Black American Negros of Freedman Descent

\#\# 1.4 MORPHOLOGICAL TYPE

  

\*\*Classification:\*\* Introflexive (root-and-pattern)

  

\*\*Root System:\*\*

  

\- \*\*Biconsonantal:\*\* C₁C₂ (less common)

\- \*\*Triconsonantal:\*\* C₁C₂C₃ (primary productive pattern)

\- \*\*Quadriconsonantal:\*\* C₁C₂C₃C₄ (complex concepts)

  

\*\*Template Morphology:\*\*

Vowel patterns applied to consonantal roots generate different meanings and parts of speech.

  

\*\*Example Root: FK (cultivate)\*\*

|CeC·u    |fek·u    |cultivator           |agent noun    |

|CoC·on   |fok·on   |cultivated thing     |patient noun  |

|CaC·ow   |fok·ow   |farm, field          |location noun |

|CāC·a    |fāk·a    |cultivate intensively|intensive verb|

|CaC·CaC·a|fak·fak·a|keep cultivating     |iterative verb|

\#\# 3.3 AAVE-INSPIRED ASPECTUAL SYSTEM

  

\#\#\# Habitual Aspect (bi-)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* bi- + Imperfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Regular, recurring action (AAVE “be doing”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*bi·mi·fak·a\* (I be cultivating = I regularly cultivate)

  

\*\*Full paradigm:\*\*

  

\- bi·mi·fak·a (I be cultivating)

\- bi·ti·fak·a (you be cultivating)

\- bi·i·fak·a (he/she be cultivating)

\- bi·ni·fak·a (we be cultivating)

\- bi·ti·fak·a·tin (y’all be cultivating)

\- bi·i·fak·a·sen (they be cultivating)

  

\#\#\# Continuative Aspect (da-)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* da- + Imperfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Ongoing, sustained action (AAVE “steady doing”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*da·mi·fak·a\* (I’m steadily cultivating)

  

\#\#\# Completive Aspect (don)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* don + Perfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Fully completed action (AAVE “done did”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*don mi·fak·im·ku\* (I done cultivated)

  

\#\#\# Remote Past (bin)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* bin + Perfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Action in distant past (AAVE “been did”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*bin mi·fak·im·ku\* (I been cultivated = I cultivated long ago)

  

\#\#\# Resultative Perfect (da·don)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* da·don + Perfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* State resulting from completed action (AAVE “been done”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*da·don mi·fak·im·ku\* (I been done cultivated = I finished and result persists)

  

\#\#\# Recent Perfect (dē-)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* dē- + Perfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Recent completion (AAVE “just did”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*dē·mi·fak·im·ku\* (I just cultivated)

  

\#\#\# Stressed Remote Phase (bin·ka)

  

\*\*Form:\*\* Imperfective + bin·ka  

\*\*Function:\*\* Long-standing state still persisting (AAVE stressed “BEEN”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*mi·fak·a bin·ka\* (I BEEN cultivating = I’ve been cultivating for a long time and still am)

\#\# 3.4 ASPECTUAL STACKING

  

\*\*Order:\*\* NEGATION - HABITUAL - CONTINUATIVE - COMPLETIVE/REMOTE - TENSE - VERB

  

\*\*Examples:\*\*

  

\- \*bi·da·mi·fak·a\* (I be steadily cultivating - habitual + continuous)

\- \*da·don mi·fak·im·ku\* (I been done cultivated - continuous + completive)

\- \*’ain·bi·da·don mi·fak·im·ku·sh\* (I ain’t be steady done cultivating)

\#\# 3.5 VOICE TRANSFORMATIONS

  

\#\#\# Active Voice

  

Base form (unmarked)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·im·a\* (to empower)

  

\#\#\# Passive Voice

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* -wi- infix between C₂ and C₃  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·\*\*wi\*\*·im·a\* (to be empowered)

  

\*\*Conjugation:\*\*

  

\- mi·sak·wi·im·a (I am empowered)

\- sak·wi·im·ku (I was empowered)

  

\#\#\# Causative Voice

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* s- prefix  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*\*\*s\*\*·sak·im·a\* (to cause to empower, to enable)

  

\*\*Conjugation:\*\*

  

\- mi·s·sak·im·a (I cause to empower)

\- s·sak·im·ku (I caused to empower)

  

\#\#\# Reflexive Voice

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* -tw suffix  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·im·\*\*tw\*\*·a\* (to empower oneself)

  

\*\*Conjugation:\*\*

  

\- mi·sak·im·tw·a (I empower myself)

\- sak·im·tw·ku (I empowered myself)

  

\#\#\# Reciprocal Voice

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* Reduplication + -tw  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·sak·im·tw·a\* (to empower each other)

  

\*\*Note:\*\* Only plural forms exist

  

\- ni·sak·sak·im·tw·a (we empower each other)

\- sak·sak·im·tw·sen (they empowered each other)

\#\# 3.6 MOOD SYSTEM

  

\#\#\# Indicative

  

Standard factual statements (base forms)

  

\#\#\# Subjunctive

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* ya- + Imperfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Wishes, hypotheticals, subordinate clauses  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*ya·mi·fak·a\* (that I might cultivate)

  

\#\#\# Imperative

  

|Form         |Pattern         |Example     |Meaning         |

|-------------|----------------|------------|----------------|

|2SG          |Bare stem       |fak·a\!      |Cultivate\!      |

|2PL          |Stem + -tin     |fak·a·tin\!  |Y’all cultivate\!|

|1PL Hortative|ni- + stem      |ni·fak·a\!   |Let’s cultivate\!|

|Negative     |en- + stem + -sh|en·fak·a·sh\!|Don’t cultivate\!|

  

\#\#\# Optative

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* me- + Imperfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Wishes, desires  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*me·mi·fak·a\* (may I cultivate / I wish I could cultivate)

  

\#\#\# Conditional

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* ko- + Imperfective  

\*\*Function:\*\* Hypothetical situations  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*ko·mi·fak·a\* (if I cultivate / I would cultivate)

  

\-----

  

\#\# 3.7 NEGATION

  

\#\#\# Standard Negation

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* en- prefix + verb + -sh suffix (double negation)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*en·mi·fak·a·sh\* (I don’t cultivate)

  

\*\*Perfective:\*\*

  

\- \*en·mi·fak·im·ku·sh\* (I didn’t cultivate)

  

\*\*Imperfective:\*\*

  

\- \*en·mi·fak·a·sh\* (I don’t cultivate)

  

\#\#\# Emphatic Negation

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* ‘ain prefix (AAVE “ain’t”)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*’ain mi·fak·im·ku\* (I ain’t cultivated)

  

\*\*With aspects:\*\*

  

\- \*’ain bi·mi·fak·a\* (I ain’t be cultivating)

\- \*’ain don mi·fak·im·ku\* (I ain’t done cultivated)

  

\#\#\# Negative Concord

  

Multiple negatives reinforce (grammatical, not canceling)  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*’ain sam·fek·u en·mi·fak·a·sh\*  

(AIN’T some·cultivator NEG·PRES·cultivate·NEG)  

“There ain’t nobody cultivating” (emphatic negation)

\#\# 3.8 COPULA VERB: WI (to be)

  

\#\#\# Zero Copula Rule

  

\*\*Present tense equational/locative sentences omit copula:\*\*

  

|English         |Soulaani    |Structure         |

|----------------|------------|------------------|

|I am good       |mi har·a    |I good            |

|You are here    |ti en·pen   |you in·this       |

|She is a teacher|i wa·fek·u·t|she a·cultivator.F|

  

\#\#\# When Copula IS Required

  

\*\*1. Past Tense:\*\*

  

\- \*mi wi·ku har·a\* (I was good)

\- \*ti wi·ki en·pen\* (you were here)

  

\*\*2. Future Tense:\*\*

  

\- \*sha·mi·wi har·a\* (I will be good)

\- \*f’na ti·wi en·pen\* (you’re finna be here)

  

\*\*3. Emphasis:\*\*

  

\- \*mi mi·wi har·a\!\* (I AM good\!)

|1PL   |wi·nun|we were   |

|3PL   |wi·sen|they were |

\#\#\# Active Participle

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* C₁aC₂·iC₃·ty  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·im·ty\* (empowering one, one who empowers)  

\*\*Declines like noun:\*\* sak·im·ty (M.SG), sak·im·ty·t (F.SG), sak·im·ty·w (M.PL)

  

\#\#\# Passive Participle

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* C₁aC₂·iC₃·wi  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·im·wi\* (empowered one)  

\*\*Declines like noun\*\*

  

\#\#\# Prospective Participle

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* C₁aC₂·iC₃·ti·ro  

\*\*Example:\*\* \*sak·im·ti·ro\* (one who will empower, future empowerer)  

\*\*Declines like noun\*\*

  

\-----

  

\# PART 4: SYNTAX

  

\#\# 4.1 BASIC WORD ORDER

  

\*\*Standard:\*\* SVO (Subject-Verb-Object)

  

\*\*Example:\*\*

\*Na·fek·u mi·fak·a na·fok·on·a\*  

(The·cultivator PRES·cultivate the·field·ACC)  

“The cultivator cultivates the field”

  

\#\# 4.2 SENTENCE TYPES

  

\#\#\# Nominal Sentences (Zero Copula)

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* SUBJECT + PREDICATE (no verb in present)

  

\*\*Examples:\*\*

  

\- \*Mi har·a\* (I \[am\] good)

\- \*Ti en·pen\* (You \[are\] here)

\- \*Pen·din Sola·din\* (Today \[is\] Sunday)

  

\#\#\# Verbal Sentences

  

Full conjugation with explicit verb

  

\*\*Examples:\*\*

  

\- \*Mi mi·fak·a na·fok·on\* (I cultivate the field)

\- \*Sen bi·i·kay·a·sen\* (They be working)

  

\#\#\# Existential Sentences

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* yit·pen + noun phrase

  

\*\*Example:\*\*

\*Yit·pen sam·fek·u·w en·na·fok·ow\*  

(It·is some·cultivator·PL in·the·farm)  

“There are some cultivators at the farm”

  

\-----

  

\#\# 4.3 QUESTION FORMATION

  

\#\#\# Yes/No Questions

  

\*\*Method 1:\*\* Rising intonation (speech) or question mark (writing)  

\*Ti har·a?\* (You good? = Are you good?)

  

\*\*Method 2:\*\* Don particle (AAVE-inspired)  

\*Don i·mi·fak·a?\* (Don’t he cultivate? = Doesn’t he cultivate?)

  

\#\#\# Wh-Questions

  

\*\*Pattern:\*\* Wh-word in situ or fronted

  

\*\*Examples:\*\*

  

\- \*Ti en·ta?\* (You \[are\] where? = Where are you?)

\- \*Ti mi·fak·a miya?\* (You cultivate what? = What do you cultivate?)

\- \*Miya ti mi·fak·a?\* (What you cultivate?)

  

\-----

  

\#\# 4.4 SUBORDINATION

  

\#\#\# Complementizer: SHA (that)

  

\*\*Example:\*\*

\*Mi kay·a sha i·f’na mi·fak·a\*  

(I know that he·FINNA cultivate)  

“I know that he’s about to cultivate”

  

\#\#\# Relative Clauses: NIN·TA (who/which)

  

\*\*Example:\*\*

\*Na·fek·u nin·ta mi·fak·a na·fok·on har·a\*  

(The·cultivator REL cultivate the·field good)  

“The cultivator who cultivates the field is good”

\# PART 6: TRANSLATION GUIDELINES

  

\#\# 6.1 ENGLISH TO SOULAANI

  

\#\#\# Step 1: Identify Sentence Type

  

\- \*\*Equational/Locative (present):\*\* Use zero copula

\- \*\*Action (present):\*\* Use imperfective conjugation

\- \*\*Action (past):\*\* Use perfective conjugation

\- \*\*Action (future):\*\* Use sha- or f’na + imperfective

  

\#\#\# Step 2: Determine Word Order

  

\- \*\*Standard:\*\* SVO (Subject-Verb-Object)

\- \*\*Questions:\*\* Wh-word can stay in situ or front

  

\#\#\# Step 3: Apply Articles

  

\- Known/specific referents: definite article (na/ta/na·pa)

\- New/unspecified referents: indefinite article (wa/sam)

  

\#\#\# Step 4: Check for AAVE Features

  

\- “be doing” → bi- prefix

\- “steady doing” → da- prefix

\- “done did” → don particle

\- “been did” → bin particle

\- “finna” → f’na particle

\- “ain’t” → ’ain particle

  

\#\#\# Step 5: Apply Case Marking

  

\- Direct objects: -a (accusative)

\- Locations: -en (locative)

\- Possessors: -i (genitive)

\- Instruments: -em (instrumental)

  

\-----

  

\#\# 6.2 SOULAANI TO ENGLISH

  

\#\#\# Step 1: Identify Verb Complex

  

\- Locate aspect/tense markers: bi-, da-, don, bin, f’na, sha-, etc.

\- Identify person prefix/suffix

\- Determine voice: -wi- (passive), s- (causative), -tw (reflexive)

  

\#\#\# Step 2: Parse Noun Phrases

  

\- Article → Root → Plural → Case → Possessive

\- Example: na·pa·fek·u·w·en·ku = the·PL·cultivator·PL·LOC·my = at my cultivators

  

\#\#\# Step 3: Handle Zero Copula

  

\- If no verb present and sentence is present tense: add “is/are/am” in English

  

\#\#\# Step 4: Translate AAVE Features Appropriately

  

\- bi- → “be \[verb\]ing” or “regularly \[verb\]”

\- da- → “steadily \[verb\]ing” or “continuously \[verb\]”

\- don → “done \[past tense\]” or “completely \[past tense\]”

\- ‘ain → “ain’t” or “definitely not”

\- f’na → “about to \[verb\]” or “finna \[verb\]”

  

\#\#\# Step 5: Check Context

  

\- Cultural expressions may not translate literally

\- Idiomatic phrases require contextual understanding

\# PART 7: CULTURAL CONTEXT

  

\#\# 7.1 Soul Land Context

  

Soulaani is the constructed language of \*\*Soul Land\*\* (Yak·in·i·Shwl·pa), a proposed 12-state regional bloc within the United States focused on African American cultural sovereignty and self-determination.

  

\*\*Core Values:\*\*

  

\- \*\*Ma·’at·i\*\* (truth, justice, cosmic order) - from Egyptian concept

\- \*\*Maf·o·i\*\* (collective work, mutual aid) - from Muskogean bee metaphor

\- \*\*Ḥit·ip·i\*\* (balance, harmony) - from Egyptian \*hetep\*

  

\*\*Cultural Practices:\*\*

  

\- \*\*Din·sal·im·i\*\* (Sunday) as communal gathering day

\- \*\*Fak·a\*\* (cultivation) as spiritual practice connecting to ancestors

\- \*\*Sal·im·ow\*\* (sanctuary, peace-place) as community center

  

\-----

\- Full case marking

\- Complete aspect marking

\- Explicit articles

\- Standard SVO order

\- AAVE features more prominent

  

\*\*Example:\*\*

\*Mek·o·pa jet·im·sen ben·’an·ah·u·w en·sal·im·ow\*  

(Leaders spoke to people at sanctuary)

  

\#\#\# Ceremonial Register

  

\- Egyptian-derived vocabulary preferred

\- Complex verbal aspects

\- Participial constructions

\- Poetic parallelism

\# PART 9: NOTES FOR AI TRANSLATION

  

\#\# 9.1 Disambiguation Rules

  

\#\#\# When Multiple Translations Possible

  

\*\*1. Zero Copula vs. Explicit Copula\*\*

  

\- \*\*Default:\*\* Use zero copula for present tense

\- \*\*Exception:\*\* Use explicit \*wi\* for emphasis or non-present tense

  

\*\*2. AAVE Aspects\*\*

  

\- \*\*Context-dependent:\*\* Translate based on English input

\- “be doing” → bi-

\- “steady” → da-

\- “done” → don

\- “been” → bin

\- “finna” → f’na

\- “gonna” / “will” → sha-

  

\*\*3. Article Selection\*\*

  

\- \*\*First mention:\*\* Indefinite (wa/sam)

\- \*\*Subsequent mention:\*\* Definite (na/ta/na·pa)

\- \*\*Generic statements:\*\* Often omit article

  

\*\*4. Case Marking\*\*

  

\- \*\*Direct objects after transitive verbs:\*\* -a (accusative)

\- \*\*Location/position:\*\* -en (locative)

\- \*\*Movement toward:\*\* Use preposition \*ben\* (no case)

\- \*\*Instrument/means:\*\* -em (instrumental)

  

\-----

  

\#\# 9.2 Error Prevention

  

\#\#\# Common Mistakes to Avoid

  

\*\*1. Don’t add copula in present tense nominal sentences\*\*

  

\- ❌ \*Mi mi·wi har·a\* (unless emphatic)

\- ✅ \*Mi har·a\*

  

\*\*2. Don’t forget person prefixes on imperfective verbs\*\*

  

\- ❌ \*fak·a\* (incomplete)

\- ✅ \*mi·fak·a\*

  

\*\*3. Don’t confuse immediate and distant future\*\*

  

\- f’na = imminent (minutes/hours)

\- sha- = later (tomorrow, plans)

  

\*\*4. Don’t omit aspect markers when translating AAVE\*\*

  

\- “I be working” ≠ “I work”

\- Must use: \*bi·mi·kay·a\* not \*mi·kay·a\*

  

\*\*5. Don’t forget plural suffixes before possessives\*\*

  

\- ❌ \*wel·du·ku\* (my child - singular only)

\- ✅ \*wel·du·w·ku\* (my children)

  

\-----

  

\#\# 9.3 Context Sensitivity

  

\#\#\# Register Detection

  

\*\*Formal contexts\*\* (official documents, ceremonies):

  

\- Use full case marking

\- Prefer Egyptian-derived vocabulary

\- Complete aspect marking

\- Standard word order

  

\*\*Casual contexts\*\* (everyday conversation):

  

\- Simplified case marking

\- AAVE features prominent

\- Articles may be dropped

\- Flexible word order

  

\*\*Indicators:\*\*

  

\- Presence of formal titles → Formal

\- AAVE features in source → Casual

\- Official/ceremonial keywords → Formal

  

\-----

  

\#\# 9.4 Handling Unknowns

  

\#\#\# When Source Word Has No Direct Translation

  

\*\*1. Check for root that covers semantic field\*\*

  

\- “car” → use \*mash·hek·om\* (walking-tool) or borrow as \*kar\*

  

\*\*2. Create compound from existing roots\*\*

  

\- “computer” → \*kiy·i·hek·om\* (thought-tool)

\- “phone” → \*jet·i·hek·om\* (speech-tool)

  

\*\*3. Use descriptive phrase\*\*

  

\- “democracy” → \*hik·em·i·na·pa·’an·ah·u·w\* (governance-of-the-people)

  

\*\*4. Borrow with Soulaani phonology\*\*

  

\- Adapt to Soulaani sound system

\- Add appropriate article and case marking
