## 1. The centerpiece: the Freedmen, named by Egypt

Egyptian has an attested word for a free person, a freedman: **nmḥ(w)**. Root **N-M-Ḥ**:

> *namiḥa* to be free · **nimeḥi** freedom · **nemaḥu** freedman · **na·pa·nemaḥwāt — THE FREEDMEN.**

The people this whole language is built for carry a name descended from the civilization the whole cosmology honors. Not borrowed from the oppressor's ledger — inherited from the Black Land's own law.

> *Anok pe nemaḥu — sanu na·pa·nemaḥwāt·i.* — "I AM a freedman — sibling of the Freedmen."

## 2. The crossing — the sentence the language owed

**ʿ-B-R**: *ʿabira* to cross; **ʿiberi** — both *crossing* and *lesson*, because in Arabic the lesson (*ʿibra*) IS the crossing. T-9 left a sentence unfinished; it completes now:

> *Bin ni·ʿabira al·baḥar·a; na·ʿiberi wi en·ib·nun·i.* — "We BEEN crossed THE Sea; the lesson lives in our hearts."
