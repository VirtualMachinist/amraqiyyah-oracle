## 1. The centerpiece: eat alone, feast together

Amraqiyyah now distinguishes **wanima** "to eat" (individual, Egyptian *wnm*) from **hamipa** "to feast, dine together" (communal, Mvskoke *hompetv*). The derivations carry the culture: **homipon** "the feast, the spread — Sunday dinner," **hamepow** "the dining place." When the weekday system lands (T-6), *Sunday dinner* gets its full name; the word is already waiting.

And beside the kitchen stands the institution: root **G-R-L** (AAVE/Southern *grill*, under R-030's cultural clause) — **geralu** "grillmaster," **garelow** "the pit, the cookout place," **gorilon** "barbecue." The cookout is heritage; now it's grammar.

## 2. The crown family: Ṭ-B-Ḫ "cook"

One Arabic root, five words, zero extra cost — the engine's best demonstration yet:

| Form | Word | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| verb | **ṭabiḫa** | to cook |
| agent | **ṭebaḫu** | a cook |
| patient | **ṭobiḫon** | a dish, cooked thing |
| abstract | **ṭibeḫi** | cooking, cuisine |
| locative | **ṭabeḫow** | **kitchen** |

Same trick, second root: **Ḫ-B-Z** "bake" → *ḫobizon* **bread**, *ḫebazu* baker, *ḫabezow* bakery.
