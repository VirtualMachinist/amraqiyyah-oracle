## R-049 · The politeness micro-set

The corpus had thank-you (*Bireki ʿala·ki!*) and nothing else of courtesy. Three roots close the gap, all Arabic-stream per the handoff's brief, all web-verified per the R-045 method:

| Root | Family | Source & verification |
|---|---|---|

Quranic root, 35 occurrences ([Wiktionary عفا](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D8%B9%D9%81%D8%A7) · [Quranic Corpus ʿ-f-w](https://corpus.quran.com/qurandictionary.jsp?q=Efw)) |

| **F-Ḍ-L** favor, grace | citation **faḍl** · *fiḍeli* grace · *faḍīl* gracious · *feḍalu* benefactor | Arabic faḍl, "grace especially unmerited"; *hāḏā min faḍli Rabbī* (Q 27:40) ([Wiktionary فضل](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D9%81%D8%B6%D9%84)) |

**The Name-pairing.** *ʿafīw* stands under **Al-ʿAfūw** — in the Way's own registry the Venus-Sirian Threshold Name on Hexagram 41, Decrease: *"the hidden release that purifies."* An apology is a decrease that purifies. Eleventh native pairing of 99, by reference to the Oracle registry as always (shared-table law).

**The two-for-one.** Arabic *ʿafwan* answers both offense and thanks; so does *ʿifewi*. The courtesy loop closes on one root: — *Bireki ʿala·ki!* — *ʿIfewi.*

**Phrases entered** (courtesy chapter): *Min faḍl·kī.* please · *ʿAfiwa·ku!* excuse me (imperative + object suffix, the *hak·il·a·ku* pattern) · *ʿIfewi.* you're welcome · *Mi·ʾisef.* I'm sorry (stative predicate, the *Mi·jiweʿ* pattern).

## The bad-slot: Ali's amendment, B-D withdrawn

The steward proposed **B-D** (AAVE *bad*, noun-converted as in "my bad," in print 1981). **Ali's ruling: no lazy English imports — carry the spirit of AAVE, refract through source language.** The construction stays (noun-converted adjective, possessed exclamation); the root material must have a true ancestor.

Verification hunt for the replacement:

- **Egyptian ḏw "evil, bad"** — attested ([Wiktionary ḏw](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E1%B8%8Fw)): adjective "evil, bad, wicked," participle of *ḏwj*. **Rejected**: the same skeleton is *mountain*, *Apep the embodiment of evil*, and the homograph of *djw* **five** — the very source of our *diyu* (R-039). Its verb-root Ḏ-W-Y lands one consonant from Ḏ-W-Q taste (*ḏawiya* / *ḏawiqa*). Too much freight for an everyday word. (The hunt also caught that [Wiktionary djw](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/djw) is the numeral — a homograph trap for future Egyptian sourcing: always fetch the lemma with diacritics.)
- **Choctaw okpulo "bad"** — Byington, live on [Webonary](https://www.webonary.org/byington-choctaw/browse/browse-english/): "ruined; spoiled; corrupted; depraved; …", the everyday Choctaw bad, "evil" in translation (*ʋni yʋt okpulo* "evil fruit"). Byington is already house style (Choctaw kin set, *haklo*, *oka*). R-017 seats Muskogean in the everyday. → **K-P-L**: *kapila* go wrong / spoil · *kipeli* fault, wrong · **kapīl** bad · stative *kipel*. **Zero lint advisories — a perfectly clean tri-root.**

**RATIFIED (R-051):** Ali concurred with the steward's recommendation — K-P-L enters ratified, and **noun-conversion by possession** becomes law (AAVE grammatical import): adjectives may be noun-converted by taking a possessive suffix directly. ***Kapīl·ku!* "My bad!"** — Choctaw root, Egyptian-style template, AAVE construction: three streams in one exclamation. *Kapīl·ku!* and *Mi·ʾisef — kapīl·ku.* enter the courtesy chapter; the gap is fully shut.

## R-050 · The two genitives (micro-ruling, confirmed by Ali)

Sample-reading the corpus surfaced a systematic split the engine didn't know:

- **Word-level case -i** (arguments; prep-governed) **merges** by R-026 sandhi: *fekwi*, *šeykū*, *en·hawtpay*.
- **The phrase-closing construct genitive ·i** (D-14's phrase-closer) is an **enclitic — always syllabic, never vowel-merged**: *sanupa·i*, *pokni·ku·i*, *mafo·ku·i*. The boundary the ruling wants audible stays audible.

Two homophonous -i's, distinguished by structure. Corpus amendment executed: *en·hawtpai* → **en·hawtpay** (that -i is the construct head's prep-governed case — word-level, merges; the old spelling was one of the handoff's "sandhi slips" caught in the open).
