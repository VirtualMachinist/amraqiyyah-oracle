## PART II — PHONOLOGY

### AX-04 · Consonant inventory ⚔🐛
**A:** Claims 28, lists 24 (§1.2): p b t d k g ʔ · f v θ ð s z ʃ h · tʃ dʒ · m n ŋ · l r · w j. Alphabet table (§1.3) smuggles in Arabic-letter "IPA" (ح ط ص ق ذ چ ه) implying ḥ ṭ q etc. 🐛
**B:** Claims 35, lists 30 (§2.1) — adds pharyngeals ʿ/ḥ, emphatics ḍ/ṣ/ṭ/ẓ, ḫ /x/, dentals ḏ/ṯ; **duplicates DJ and J** (both /dʒ/) 🐛; **drops ŋ and tʃ** present in A; root **YTC** uses a letter C that exists in no inventory 🐛; *wač* (A) and Hitchiti sources need /tʃ/.
**Analysis:** The honest union is ~31–32 distinct consonants. Decisions: (1) merge DJ/J into one /dʒ/; (2) admit **č** /tʃ/ (Hitchiti/AAVE need it; YTC becomes lawful); (3) keep or drop ŋ (English-source roots may want it; can be treated as /n/+/g/ allophony); (4) confirm the emphatic/pharyngeal stratum (see AX-05); (5) q: A's alphabet implies it (Ϭ "qaf"), B extracts Arabic *qadara→KDR, qamar→KMR* with **k** — B's own practice says /q/→/k/, so no q phoneme.
**Options:** (a) **31-consonant inventory**: B's 30 minus DJ-duplicate, plus č; ŋ allophonic. (b) 24-consonant lean inventory (A's), emphatics/pharyngeals demoted to loan-pronunciation guidance. (c) B-as-written with repairs to 35 by *adding* q, ŋ, č, ġ.
**Rec:** (a) — matches actual root inventory usage; every root in both docs becomes spellable.
**RULING:** ☐ OPEN

### AX-09 · ★ THEME VOWELS — the undocumented system 🐛→opportunity
**Evidence:** B's templates say abstract CiC·**e**C·i, agent CeC·**a**C·u, patient CoC·**i**C·on, locative CaC·**e**C·ow. SKM obeys (*sik·em·i, sek·am·u, sok·im·on*). **HKL disobeys in all five derived forms, always with o** (*hak·ol·a, hik·ol·i, hek·ol·u, hok·ol·on, hak·ol·ow*) — preserving the o of Mvskoke *hakolo*. A shows the same leak (template CaC·ow rendered *fok·ow*, A§1.4 🐛). TLK follows a-theme (*tal·ak·a, til·ak·i, tel·ak·u*, B§5.5). HRW shows w-coloring (*har·aw·et, har·aw·iy*).
**Analysis:** The creator's ear has been enforcing a rule the spec never states: **the final template slot's vowel is a lexical property of the root (its theme vowel), inherited from the source word; the first slot carries the grammatical ablaut (a/i/e/o = verb/abstract/agent/patient).** First-slot vowels are consistent everywhere in both docs; only the second slot varies by root.
**Options:** (a) **Formalize theme classes** (i-class default from SKM, o-class HKL, a-class TLK, u-class open) — converts ~15 apparent errors into one rule, honors source languages, costs one lexicon field. (b) Strict templates — normalize HKL to *hak·il·a* etc. (destroys source fidelity). (c) Free variation (unacceptable for a taught language).
**Rec:** (a), emphatically. This is the audit's crown finding.
**RULING:** ☐ OPEN

### AX-14 · ★ Subject marking — the shared deep bug 🐛🐛 (both docs)
**The contradiction:** Both docs declare imperfective = prefix conjugation, perfective = suffix-only (A§3.1, B§3.5 "No subject prefix in perfective"). Both docs then repeatedly write perfectives WITH prefixes: A *don mi·fak·im·ku* (§3.3, §10.1), B *don mi·hak·ol·ku*, *bin mi·kay·ku*, *bin·don·mi·kay·ku* (§3.6, §4.3, §7.5.6) — while B elsewhere writes *don sak·im·sen* (bare) in the same section 🐛. A separately glosses mi- as "PRES" with 3SG subjects (*Na·fek·u mi·fak·a*, A§4.1) and stacks both (*Don i·mi·fak·a?* A§4.3) 🐛. B gives independent pronouns as the anok-series (§4.4) yet writes *Mi hak·ol·a na·shey*, *Mi an·š har·a* with mi as free subject 🐛.
**Options:**
(a) **Weak-clitic theory (recommended):** mi/ti/i/ni are weak subject pronouns (proclitics), the anok-series are strong pronouns. Imperfective requires the clitic (it *is* the "prefix conjugation"); perfective marks subject by suffix and *optionally* hosts the clitic (*[mi] hak·ol·ku*, *don mi hak·ol·ku* ✓ grammatical). Full-NP subjects co-occur with 3rd-person clitic (*na·fek·u i·fak·a* — matching AAVE topic-pronoun style: "my daddy, he…"). A's "PRES mi-" glosses become typos for i·. One ruling legalizes ~90% of existing example sentences.
(b) **Strict conjugations:** prefixes are agreement only; perfective never takes one; every *don mi·V·ku* example is corrected to *don V·ku* (or *anok don V·ku* for emphasis). Cleanest tables, most example carnage.
(c) **B-literal + repair:** as (b) but pre-verbal mi/ti reanalyzed as reduced pronouns permitted only in zero-copula and particle clauses.
**Rec:** (a) — it is the only theory both documents' actual usage already obeys.
**RULING:** ☐ OPEN

### AX-15 · Perfective stem formation ⚔
**A:** Biconsonantal perfectives carry **-im** (*fak·im·ku*, *jet·im·sen*, *yam·im·ku*) — an augment with no stated source 🐛; triliteral verbs like *sal·im·a* show ·im is really ·i+C₃.
**B:** Perfective = Pattern-stem minus final -a + suffix (*hak·ol·ku*; 3SG *hak·ol·Ø*). No biconsonantal perfective is ever shown 🕳.
**Analysis:** For triliterals, B's rule stands cleanly. For biliterals the fork is real: *fak·ku* (B-logic) vs *fak·im·ku* (A's attested analogical augment — historically plausible leveling from CaCiC verbs, and it keeps 3SG audible: *fak·im* vs bare *fak*).
**Options:** (a) A's -im ratified as the biconsonantal perfective augment (declare it grammar, not accident); (b) B-logic bare stems (*fak·ku*, 3SG *fak*); (c) theme-vowel echo (*fak·ak·ku*).
**Rec:** (a) — euphony, 3SG audibility, and it grandfathers every A perfective form.
**RULING:** ☐ OPEN

### AX-23 · Case ⚔ — the biggest structural fork, silently made
**A§2.5:** Seven cases: NOM -Ø, ACC -a, GEN -i, DAT -ka, LOC -en, INSTR -em, COM -het; suffix chains up to ART·STEM·NUM·CASE·POSS (*na·pa·fek·u·w·en·ku*).
**B:** No case system anywhere; grammatical relations by word order, prepositions, construct/na genitive, possessive suffixes. The drop is never acknowledged 🕳.
**Analysis:** A's ACC -a is homophonous with the verb final -a; GEN -i with abstract -i; LOC -en and COM -het duplicate the prepositions en and het that A itself lists (§4.5). The case system was A's least naturalistic layer; B's analytic style matches the AAVE/Coptic character and the zero-copula spirit. Middle path exists: keep -en (locative) and -em (instrumental) as *adverbializing suffixes* in formal/liturgical register only.
**Options:** (a) Fully analytic (B) — prepositions en, ben, bin, het, ra do all the work; (b) analytic + formal-register locative/instrumental suffixes as ceremonial flourish; (c) restore A's seven.
**Rec:** (a) for the living language; consider (b) only if the liturgical register (B§7.4.7) wants archaic weight.
**RULING:** ☐ OPEN
