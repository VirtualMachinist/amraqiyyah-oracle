## 1. The centerpiece: the Heart-Idiom System

Egyptian expressed emotion through the **ib** — the heart-mind ratified at T-3. Joy was *ꜣwt-ib*, "wideness of heart." Amraqiyyah grammaticalizes the pattern:

> **ib + stative/adjective = emotion predicate.**
> *Ib·ku wasīʿ.* — "My heart is wide" = I am overjoyed.
> *Ib·to ḥizen.* — "His heart grieves."
> *Ib·nun ḥitep.* — "Our hearts are at rest" = we are content (ḥitep — a **freebie from the ratified Ḥ-T-P root**: contentment was in the lexicon all along).
> *Ib šajīʿ* — a brave heart.

The idiom inventory grows in the phrasebook; the grammatical pattern ratifies here. And *wasīʿ* "wide" is **Al-Wāsiʿ** — the first emotion idiom already touches the 99 Names.
