## 1. The centerpiece: the Two Hearts

Egyptian distinguishes **jb** — the heart as mind, will, and conscience, the heart weighed against Ma'at's feather — from **ḥꜣty**, the anatomical organ. Amraqiyyah imports the full doctrine:

> **ib** = heart-mind, conscience, seat of intention (*ib·ku har·āy* "my heart is good") · **ḥati** = the heart that beats (*ḥati·to*, "his heart [the organ]").

For a language whose Oracle stands on Ma'at, the weighing-heart is not vocabulary — it is infrastructure. (T-7 will build on it: *ib* + Ma'at idioms, the examined conscience.)

## 4. Proposed rule extension (R-035): body parts join the inalienable class

Classic typology — and Muskogean practice — treats **kin and body parts as one inalienable class**. Extension of R-033's soft law: body-part terms in referential use normally carry their possessor (*ḏart·ku* "my hand," *ib·to* "his heart"); bare forms for the generic/anatomical (*na·ib* "the heart [in general]"), vocative-poetic, and citation uses.
