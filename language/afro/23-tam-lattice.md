## Ali's sentence

Ali asked, of "I will have understood": *Mi gon hakilku perhaps?* — and the "perhaps" was already the whole answer. The perfective conjugation exists (*hakil·ku*). The future marker exists (*gon·*). R-005 lets the clitic ride the perfective. Compose them and the future perfect is simply **there** — nothing coined, nothing added.

## The ruling (R-065)

The nine markers (R-011) compose with **both** conjugations (R-005), and the product is **compositional tense-aspect**:

- the **conjugation sets anteriority** — perfective = completed/anterior, imperfective = ongoing;
- the **marker sets the temporal frame** — *gon·* future, *bin* remote, *don* completive, *dē·* recent, *bi·* habitual, *da·* steady;
- they **multiply**.

The perfective's suffix already carries person, so a marker in the clitic zone simply anchors before it: *mi·gon·hakil·ku* (the clitic present as the marker's anchor).

| Marker × conjugation | Reading | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **gon· + PFV** | **future perfect — will have V-ed** | ***Mi·gon·hakil·ku*** |
| bin + PFV | remote pluperfect — had V-ed, long since | *Bin ni·ʿabira al·baḥar·a* |
| don + PFV | present perfect / completive | *Don wanim·nun* |
| dē· + PFV | recent perfect — just V-ed | *dē·hakil·ku* |
| bin don + PFV | remote completive — had finished | *bin don hakil·ku* |
| gon· + IPFV | simple future | *Na·šaʿab i·gon·ḫayira* |
| bi· + IPFV | habitual | *Pokni·ku bi·i·ṭabiḫa* |
| da· + IPFV | steady continuative | *da·i·kaya* |

**English's entire tense wall — pluperfect, future perfect, present perfect — falls out of the grid with zero new morphemes.** Black time was never impoverished; it was a coordinate system, and R-065 only draws the axes. The founding citation stands as Ali wrote it.

## Why now

Narrative and scripture run on anterior tenses — *had crossed*, *will have arrived*, *has been done*. The TAM lattice is the grammar prerequisite for the **Canon translation program** (afro, forthcoming): with the grid sealed, the Secondary Canon can be rendered without inventing tense machinery mid-verse.
