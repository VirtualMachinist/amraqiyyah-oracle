## 1. The centerpiece: Black, from the Black Land

The color **black** is **kamāy** — root **K-M**, Egyptian *km*, the root of **Kemet, "the Black Land"** itself. The people's self-designation and the language's word for black descend from the same soil the whole cosmology honors. Beside it, the Egyptian color-pair completes: **dašīr** red (the Red Land, *dšrt*) — and the Two Lands live on as a color wheel. Blue is **zarīq** (the color; *bluz* remains the feeling), yellow **ṣafīr**, brown **samīr** — from Arabic *asmar*, an *endearing* register for warm-dark skin, never a slur; skin-tone vocabulary enters with honor. Green was already ours (*ḫaḍīr*, T-4); white (*hokta*) stays pending with the Muskogean queue.
