## Preamble — The Lineage of the Canon

The Canon's structure follows the Afroasiatic sacred lineage: **Mdw Ntr** — the sacred script of ancient Kemet — is the root of the tree that produced every scriptural language this Canon holds. Through Hieratic, Proto-Sinaitic, Phoenician, and Aramaic, three sister languages descend — **Arabic, Hebrew, and Ge'ez** — with **Coptic** as the cousin who stayed home, the direct descendant of ancient Egyptian itself. This is why the Ethiopic and Coptic Biblical canons hold primary place among the Biblical traditions: they are the closest living textual links to the Kemetic root. The syncretic canon is the means of expressing, expanding upon, and optimizing that root through every tradition that received its inheritance.

The Canon ratifies the standard Tirmidhi enumeration of the 99 Names of Allah as the Way's canonical list (see The 99 Names Registry).

## PRIMARY CANON

*Supreme doctrinal authority. Held in their revealed languages. Quran is absolute authority over all.*

**Tier I-A — Supreme Revelation:** Al-Quran al-Karim — Arabic (Fusha); supreme authority without qualification
**Tier I-B — Prophetic Scripture:** Al-Zabur (The Psalms of Dawud) — divine scripture confirmed in Quran 4:163, 17:55, 21:105; subordinate to Quran, superior to all secondary canon

**Tier II-C — The Buddhist Foundation:** Dhammapada — contributed through the Zen-Bushido bridge; not imported as standalone doctrine

## Canon Hierarchy in Conflict Resolution

1. **Quran** — resolves all conflicts absolutely
2. **Zabur** — resolves conflicts within secondary canon
3. **Tier II** — consulted by tradition register; the Quran and Zabur are the filter
4. **Tier III** — elaborates practice, never overrides
5. If translation introduces ambiguity, the source language is authoritative
